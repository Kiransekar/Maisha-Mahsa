## 0. Versioning (mechanism, not legal content)

Same mechanism as the ToS/Privacy/DPA (see `TOS_DRAFT.md` §0): a published version is an entry
in `app.core.legal.PUBLISHED` (`doc_type="dpdp_notice"`); acceptance is a `legal_acceptance`
row; a version bump requires re-acceptance, tracked per-user (`needs_reacceptance`). Until a
counsel-approved version is published, `PUBLISHED` carries no `dpdp_notice` entry, the
acceptance gates (`legal.require_current_acceptance` at onboarding and employee-data
ingestion) are no-ops, and the product honestly reports "no notice published".

## 3. Legal hold (erasure vs books retention)

An erasure request that touches records still under the statutory books-of-account retention
duty — Companies Act 2013 s.128(5), "not less than eight financial years", as sourced in
`api/tests/statutory_oracle/vectors/ws1c_proven_defects.yaml` and computed by the vault
retention machinery (§WS1.C5, 8 years from FY-end) — is placed in state `held` with that basis
recorded. Retained records are **never deleted while the duty runs**; the request can complete
only once retention lapses. `TODO(counsel)`: confirm the DPDP-side carve-out wording (retention
"necessary for compliance with any law") and whether any record classes need different
treatment.
