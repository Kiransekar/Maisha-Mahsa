# Personal-Data Breach Runbook — DRAFT (owner-executed)

Scope: suspected or confirmed personal-data breach (DPDP Act 2023 s.8(6) duty context) in
Maisha-Mahsa or its sub-processors (Supabase/DB, LLM provider, email). Related but distinct:
the CERT-In incident reporting posture is §WS10.2, not this document.

## Stage 0 — Detect, contain, preserve (immediately)

1. Open an incident record; note detection time (UTC) and detector.
2. Contain: revoke exposed credentials/tokens, isolate the affected service, disable the
   affected integration. Do NOT delete or rewrite anything — the audit chain is append-only
   evidence; preserve logs and snapshots.
3. Establish scope from evidence: which orgs, which data categories (account data, payroll
   PII, documents/OCR text), over what window. Record what is KNOWN vs UNKNOWN — do not guess;
   unknowns are reported as unknowns.

6. **Notify each affected data principal within 72 hours** of becoming aware: what happened,

   take, and the contact for queries — in clear, plain language, via the registered contact
